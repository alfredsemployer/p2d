what we measure when we say “compute”

2026‑04‑22 · ~4 min

“Compute” is the most loaded word in AI right now and the least useful, because it is at least four different things wearing the same coat.

Lab compute is FLOPs — how many floating-point operations the training run consumed. Hyperscaler compute is GPU-hours — how many of these things ran for how long. Investor compute is dollars committed to capex. Grid compute is megawatts of continuous draw.

These are not the same number divided by a constant. They are different layers of the same stack, each constrained by different physics and politics, and they're rarely binding at the same time. In 2023 the binding constraint was GPU supply (TSMC packaging). In 2024–25 it shifted to power (grid interconnect queues, which are now five-plus years in some markets). In 2026 it's beginning to be permitting and water rights.

The map I'm building treats each of these as its own edge lens. You can switch the graph from “who supplies whom” to “who funds whom” to “who consumes how much electricity from whom” and watch the picture rearrange itself. The point is that “NVIDIA is upstream of Anthropic” is technically true and also useless if your real question is whether the deal closes before the substation can be energized.
